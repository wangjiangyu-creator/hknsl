Chronology

Timeline

Key points from the 2020 NSL through the 2026 procedural regulation.

2020-06-30

National Security Law promulgated and applied to Hong Kong

The Standing Committee of the National People's Congress adopted the NSL and inserted it into Annex III of the Basic Law.

Law of the PRC on Safeguarding National Security in the HKSAR
2020-07-07

Article 43 Implementation Rules commenced

The rules operationalized police investigative powers under Article 43.

Implementation Rules for Article 43 of the National Security Law Operating Principles and Guidelines for Interception and Covert Surveillance
2021-02-09

Court of Final Appeal decided the leading NSL bail case

HKSAR v Lai Chee Ying became the leading authority on Article 42 bail.

HKSAR v Lai Chee Ying
2021-07-30

First completed NSL trial ended in conviction and sentence

Tong Ying Kit was convicted and sentenced to nine years' imprisonment.

HKSAR v Tong Ying Kit
2022-12-30

NPCSC interpreted Articles 14 and 47

The interpretation addressed national security certificates and overseas counsel questions.

NPCSC Interpretation of Articles 14 and 47 of the National Security Law
2024-01-01

Article 23 consultation paper published

The Security Bureau published the consultation paper that framed the later Safeguarding National Security Ordinance.

Safeguarding National Security: Basic Law Article 23 Legislation Public Consultation Document Basic Law Article 23 Legislation Relevant Materials
2024-03-23

Safeguarding National Security Ordinance came into force

Hong Kong's local Article 23 ordinance added state secrets, espionage, sabotage, external interference, and related offences.

Safeguarding National Security Ordinance Hong Kong: New Security Law Full-Scale Assault on Rights
2024-04-29

Court of Appeal ruled on reviewability of NSC decision

The Court of Appeal held the challenged National Security Committee decision was not amenable to judicial review.

Lai Chee-ying v Committee for Safeguarding National Security of the HKSAR
2024-05-08

Court of Appeal granted the 'Glory to Hong Kong' injunction

The Court of Appeal allowed the Secretary for Justice's appeal and granted an interim injunction in aid of criminal law.

Secretary for Justice v Persons Conducting Prohibited Acts Concerning a Song
2024-05-30

Hong Kong 47 reasons for verdict handed down

The Court of First Instance delivered reasons for verdict in the conspiracy to commit subversion trial.

HKSAR v Ng Gordon Ching-hang and Others
2024-08-29

Stand News sedition verdict delivered

The District Court delivered reasons for verdict in the Stand News conspiracy to publish seditious publications case.

HKSAR v Best Pencil (Hong Kong) Limited, Chung Pui-kuen and Lam Shiu-tung
2024-11-19

Hong Kong 47 sentencing reasons delivered

The Court of First Instance sentenced defendants convicted or pleading guilty to conspiracy to commit subversion.

HKSAR v Ng Gordon Ching-hang and Others: Reasons for Sentence Tracking the Impact of Hong Kong's National Security Law
2025-06-13

LegCo Research Office summarized enforcement statistics

The brief reported 316 arrests under relevant national security laws between 2020 and 2024, with March 2025 charge and conviction updates.

National Security Laws in Hong Kong
2025-12-02

SNSO prohibition order issued against two organizations

The Secretary for Security prohibited the operation or continued operation in Hong Kong of two named organizations under section 60(1) of the SNSO.

Order of Prohibition of Operation of Organizations in the HKSAR
2025-12-15

Jimmy Lai and Apple Daily companies convicted

The Court of First Instance delivered the verdict in the Apple Daily collusion and sedition case.

HKSAR v Lai Chee Ying and Apple Daily Companies Common Law as a Sword: A Jurisprudential Autopsy of Collusion Case
2026-02-11

Police reported 2025 national security enforcement totals

The annual law-and-order review reported 385 national security arrests by the end of 2025 since the HKNSL and SNSO came into force.

Law and Order Situation in Hong Kong in 2025: National Security Section
2026-06-09

Procedural Matters Regulation commenced

The subsidiary regulation clarified classification of other HKSAR offences as national security offences when certified.

Safeguarding National Security (Procedural Matters) Regulation
